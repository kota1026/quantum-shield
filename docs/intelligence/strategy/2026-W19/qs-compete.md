# qs-compete — Competitive Positioning (W19, 2026-05-09)

## 1. Position

QS's defensible wedge is **"the PQC attestation + custody primitive that institutional MPC custodians plug into, anchored on Ethereum L1 and Japan-regulated rails."** Not a standalone custody product competing with Fireblocks — a **dual-signature attestation layer (Dilithium + SPHINCS+) with on-chain proof that any custodian, exchange, or CBDC pilot can integrate**. Japan-rooted is a *distribution* moat (FSA, NTT, defense procurement), not a *technology* moat — don't confuse the two.

## 2. Three concrete actions (next 1 week)

1. **Publish `docs/competitive/QS_vs_LANDSCAPE.md`** — a one-page matrix: QS vs QRL (own-chain trap), Algorand (Falcon roadmap, no custody), Fireblocks/BitGo (MPC, no PQC shipped), QANplatform (PQC-claim, no NIST-final algorithms), SandboxAQ (consulting, not protocol). Cross-post to ethresear.ch *and* the EF ESP grant thread. Frame: **"dual-signature on EVM L1 is the only shipped path; everyone else is roadmap."** This anchors the narrative before competitors do.

2. **Cold-outreach 3 institutional custody BDs (Fireblocks, BitGo, Anchorage) with a "PQC attestation co-pilot" deck.** Pitch QS as the *signature layer they bolt onto MPC*, not a replacement. MPC distributes key risk; QS distributes *algorithm* risk. The two compose. If even one engages, QS converts a competitor into a channel.

3. **Initiate NTT R&D + NEC Quantum Safe Network contact via Japan ETH community (Fracton, Stake Technologies).** Goal: a co-authored whitepaper by end of Q3, not a partnership announcement. Co-authorship is cheaper than a deal and harder for US-coastal competitors to replicate. Reference `docs/grants/EF_ESP_APPLICATION.md` for framing.

## 3. One competitive risk

**Fireblocks or Anchorage shipping a "ML-DSA co-signing" feature in H2 2026 — even a thin wrapper — kills QS's institutional wedge.** They have the client base; QS has the protocol. If they ship first and frame MPC+PQC as "complete," QS becomes a research curiosity. Mitigation: **ship the attestation-as-a-service SDK before they do, and get one named institutional pilot on the website by end of Q3.** Speed-to-integration > algorithmic elegance.

## 4. Non-obvious geopolitical / commercial angle

**China BSN is mandating SM2/SM3 successors and will not adopt NIST FIPS 204/205.** This bifurcates the global PQC stack into a NIST bloc and a Chinese bloc. Japan, Korea, Taiwan, ASEAN, EU, and India will sit on the NIST side — and Japan is uniquely positioned as the *NIST-aligned APAC anchor* with both regulatory trust (FSA) and quantum R&D depth (NTT, Toshiba, RIKEN). **US-coastal competitors view Japan as a small market; they miss that Japan is the credentialing gateway for the entire NIST-bloc APAC custody market post-2027.** QS being JP-domiciled, NIST-aligned, and EVM-native is a three-way intersection no US or UK competitor can cheaply replicate. Lean into it as *regulatory geography*, not nationalism.
