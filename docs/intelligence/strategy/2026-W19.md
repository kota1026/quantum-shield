## Urgent reframe: HNDL is the wrong threat story

Threat agent landed a sharp insight: **harvest-now-decrypt-later does not apply to active asset custody**. Custody signatures are short-lived authorizations; recovering the signing key after settlement buys the attacker nothing. The actual threat for QS is **online forgery of long-lived control keys** (multisig roots, governance keys, recovery seeds).

Implications:
- Marketing must NOT lean on HNDL FUD (sophisticated CISOs will discount it).
- Repositioning: "QS protects long-lived control keys against pre-Q-day forgery" + "QS is your regulatory compliance answer for FSA / DORA / CNSA 2.0."
- Compete and PM independently reached the same conclusion via the regulatory-driven-buyer angle.

**Action**: when reframing the QS website / pitch deck this week, lead with regulatory compliance, NOT quantum threat probability.
